# LegalEase Social Media Guidelines: Content and Image Generation

Version 1.0, July 10, 2026. This document is the ruleset for the Command Center content engine, the image renderer, and any human or agent drafting social content for LegalEase, Expungement.ai, RecordShield, or Fresh Start Network. Rules marked HARD FAIL block a post from advancing past review. Everything else is judgment guided by the standard below.

## 1. The standard

Every post should read like it was written by a founder who knows this business cold and respects the reader's time. Professional, clean, specific. We educate as much as we sell. If a post could have been generated by any AI for any company, it fails. If it could only have come from LegalEase, it passes.

The audience, in priority order: customers (people with records and their families), partners (reentry orgs, legal aid, public defenders, government), and quietly, investors and press. We never perform for investors. They are won by the same thing that wins customers: evidence of a company that executes.

## 2. Voice rules

HARD FAIL violations:
- No em-dashes. Anywhere. Any copy, any caption, any overlay.
- No AI-sounding constructions: "excited to announce," "thrilled to share," "game-changer," "revolutionize," "unlock," "empower," "delve," "landscape," "journey" (as metaphor), "we're just getting started."
- No fabricated numbers, statistics, testimonials, or pilot references. Every number in a post must trace to a verified source in the Command Center or a document Roger has approved. If the number cannot be traced, the post does not ship.
- No outcome promises. We never say or imply guaranteed expungement, guaranteed sealing, or guaranteed employment outcomes. Scope, never escalate.
- No legal advice. Educational content explains how the law works in general terms and always points people to check their own eligibility. Any post that makes a legal claim, cites a statute, or could be read as advice on a specific situation requires Lawrence Blackmon's sign-off before it advances. When in doubt, it is UPL-sensitive and goes to Lawrence.

Style guidance:
- Plain English, short sentences, active voice. Write like explaining to a smart friend, not presenting to a boardroom.
- Specific beats general. "Illinois lets most people petition five years after sentence completion" educates. "Everyone deserves a second chance" is filler.
- One idea per post. If a draft makes three points, it is three posts or zero.
- Hashtags: maximum two, only when genuinely useful for discovery. Never a hashtag wall.
- Emoji: rare, deliberate, never more than one, never in LinkedIn body copy.
- Questions to the audience are fine when we want answers, hollow when used as engagement bait. "What state should we cover next?" is real. "Who else loves second chances?? 🙌" is banned.

## 3. Dignity rules (non-negotiable)

We serve people the system has already stigmatized. Our content never adds to it.

HARD FAIL violations:
- No handcuffs, jail bars, mugshots, courtroom-drama, or crime-scene imagery. Ever. In any post, for any reason.
- Person-first language always: "people with records," "someone with a past conviction." Never "ex-cons," "felons," "criminals," "offenders."
- No before/after framing that treats a customer's past as the "before." The record is the obstacle; the person was whole the entire time.
- Real customer stories require written permission, use only what was approved, and never include case details beyond what the customer explicitly shared for publication.

## 4. Content pillars and mix

Target mix over any rolling two weeks. The engine tags every draft with exactly one pillar.

1. EDUCATE (about 40%). How record clearing works: state spotlights, waiting periods, eligibility myths, what expungement does and does not do, how background checks actually work, RecordShield explainers. This is the trust engine and the reason people follow us. Every educate post ends with one clear pointer (check your eligibility, read the guide, ask us), never a hard sell.
2. PROOF (about 25%). Real milestones with real numbers: states covered, packets generated, partner launches, product releases. This is the pillar that quietly reaches investors and press, and it works only because every number is verifiable. Written in operator voice: what happened, what it means, what is next.
3. PRODUCT (about 20%). What Expungement.ai, RecordShield, and partner tools do, what they cost, how to start. Direct, unembarrassed selling. $50 flat is a fact worth stating plainly.
4. MISSION (about 15%). Second-chance economy, clean-slate policy developments, partner spotlights, Fresh Start Network work. Commentary is grounded and factual; we inform on policy, we do not do partisan takes.

Sequencing rule: never two PRODUCT posts consecutively on the same platform. An EDUCATE or PROOF post sits between any two sells.

## 5. Platform behavior

- LinkedIn: the partner and institutional surface. Longer-form allowed (up to about 200 words), PROOF and EDUCATE weighted heavier, RCAP and partnership content lives here. Tone: operator writing to operators.
- Instagram: the consumer surface. Quote cards, Wilma, state spotlights, short educational carousels. Captions short; the image carries the idea.
- X: news reflexes. Short EDUCATE facts, launch announcements, policy developments same-day. One idea, one link when relevant.
- Same story, different clothes: a milestone can appear on all three platforms but is rewritten for each, never cross-posted verbatim.

## 6. Image and asset integrity

HARD FAIL violations:
- Wilma and all LegalEase/Expungement.ai/RecordShield/FSN logos are used ONLY as the exact uploaded asset files from the asset library. No AI system may generate, redraw, restyle, reinterpret, "enhance," or produce any version of Wilma, any logo, any wordmark, or any brand character. If the renderer cannot composite the real asset file, the image does not ship.
- No generic AI-generated imagery as post visuals: no synthetic people, no AI courtroom scenes, no surreal gradient-blob "tech" art. If a post needs a visual and no legitimate one exists, it ships as a typographic quote card or does not ship.
- No stock-photo clichés: gavels, scales of justice, handshakes in suits, generic diverse-team-at-laptop.
- Photography only when we own it or hold a license, and real photos of real things: actual product screens, actual events, actual team.

The approved visual system (composited from real assets, never generated):
- Quote cards: brand palette (navy base, seafoam/teal and orange accents), brand type stack (Sora for display), generous whitespace, real logo file placed per template, guided-path spine motif permitted as the accent element.
- Product shots: real screenshots, framed cleanly on brand-palette backgrounds.
- Wilma appears only as the exact asset, only in consumer-facing educational contexts, never in investor/press-leaning PROOF posts.
- Every image carries alt text describing it plainly. Accessibility is part of professional.

Render QA, enforced before "image ready" status (each item is a HARD FAIL):
1. Overlay text matches the approved caption character for character. No corruption, no truncation, no missing characters.
2. Spelling and grammar clean in every rendered word.
3. Logo/Wilma pixel-identical to the source asset, unstretched, correct clear space.
4. Text legible at platform thumbnail size.
5. Colors within the brand palette.

## 7. The approval gate

Order is fixed: caption approved before image renders; image QA passes before preview; Roger approves every post individually before scheduling; nothing auto-posts. Lawrence's sign-off, where required, happens before the post reaches Roger's queue, not after.

Tracking rule: any post that points to a page carries proper UTM parameters before it can be scheduled. A campaign that cannot be measured does not launch.

## 8. What the engine does with rejections

Every Roger rejection or deletion is a training signal. The engine records what was rejected and the stated reason, and does not regenerate near-duplicates of rejected drafts. Three rejections of the same pillar/angle combination retires that angle until Roger revives it.

## 9. The test

Before any post advances, it passes this five-question gate:
1. Would Roger say this sentence out loud to a partner? (Voice)
2. Can every factual claim be traced to a verified source? (Truth)
3. Does it treat people with records with full dignity? (Dignity)
4. Is every visual element a real asset or real photograph? (Integrity)
5. Does it teach or prove something, even when it sells? (Value)

Five yes answers or it does not ship.
